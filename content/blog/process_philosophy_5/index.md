This lecture is a conversation with Ed Kelly as a guest, coming from university of virginia, department of perceptual studies. The discussion focuses on non-local or immaterial phenemena like 'Psi', which honestly seemed better addressed in documentaires like the Telepahty Tapes, and while such miraculous phenemena are very much my area of interest I found this conversation a bit fragmented and a yawn.  

In terms of Whitehead they cover old ground of actual entities as having a physical and mental dipole, and that this is held together through prehension. I suppose when you get into non-locality and psi, prehension is a pretty good term. The most salient aspect of the discussion for me was the mention of 'eternal objects' and 'potenti', the former being along the lines of platonic forms, the latter being less platonic, so probably closer to Wolframs hypergraphs or something like that.
